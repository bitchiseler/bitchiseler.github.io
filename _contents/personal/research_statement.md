# Research Statement

## Introduction

My research focuses on the intersection of embedded systems, robotics middleware, and high-performance computing. I work to bridge the gap between academic system modeling and production-grade embedded platforms, with particular emphasis on system reliability and performance optimization under real-world constraints.

## Research Interests

### Embedded & Real-Time Systems
My work in embedded systems addresses fundamental challenges in system reliability and performance optimization. This includes:

- **Linux Kernel Development**: Device driver implementation, system bring-up, and power management optimization for ARM-based platforms
- **Real-Time Performance**: Analysis and optimization of timing constraints in embedded systems
- **System Reliability**: Design of fault-tolerant systems that maintain operation under adverse conditions

### Robotics Middleware
In robotics, I focus on middleware integration and real-time communication:

- **ROS 2 Architecture**: Integration of ROS 2 with embedded platforms and real-time constraints
- **UAV Systems**: Development of control architectures for unmanned aerial vehicles
- **Communication Systems**: Real-time data streaming over wireless networks, including 5G integration

### High-Performance Computing
My research in parallel computing addresses scalability challenges:

- **GPU Acceleration**: CUDA-based implementation of computationally intensive algorithms
- **Distributed Systems**: OpenMPI-based distributed simulation architectures
- **Performance Modeling**: Empirical measurement and analytical modeling of system performance

## Research Methodology

My research approach combines theoretical analysis with practical implementation:

1. **System Architecture Design**: High-level design of robust, scalable system components
2. **Empirical Validation**: Performance measurement and system profiling under realistic conditions
3. **Prototyping**: Rapid development of proof-of-concept implementations
4. **Collaboration**: Interdisciplinary work with domain specialists

## Current Research Directions

### Embedded Linux System Optimization
Current work focuses on production-grade embedded systems:

- Power management and thermal optimization for commercial certification
- System stability and reproducibility in long-term deployments
- Integration of commercial middleware with open-source platforms

### Robotics Middleware Integration
Ongoing research in robotics systems:

- ROS 2 and ArduPilot integration for UAV control systems
- Real-time video streaming and communication over private 5G networks
- System-level debugging and performance optimization in constrained environments

### Parallel Computing Applications
Continued work in high-performance computing:

- GPU-accelerated traffic simulation for urban planning applications
- Distributed simulation architectures for large-scale scenarios
- Performance measurement and scalability analysis

## Research Impact

My work contributes to both academic knowledge and industrial practice:

- **Academic Contributions**: Publications in peer-reviewed journals and conferences
- **Industrial Applications**: Commercial systems deployed in production environments
- **Open Source**: Contributions to open-source projects and community resources

## Future Research Plans

### Long-Term Goals
- Development of explainable embedded systems with predictable behavior
- Integration of formal methods with practical embedded system design
- Advancement of robotics middleware for safety-critical applications

### Collaborative Opportunities
I seek collaboration in:
- Embedded system reliability and security
- Robotics middleware optimization
- High-performance computing applications
- Academic-industry partnerships

## Conclusion

My research addresses fundamental challenges in embedded systems and robotics through a combination of theoretical analysis and practical implementation. By bridging academic research with industrial applications, I aim to contribute to the development of more reliable, efficient, and scalable computing systems.
